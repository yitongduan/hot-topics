GMO food

Any new application of science and technology has its two sides. The development and utilization of nuclear energy has provided enormous nuclear energy for human beings and also created nuclear weapons with great destructive effects on human beings; the application of pesticides has played a huge role in the prevention and control of crop pests, which has greatly increased crop production, but also caused great harm to human beings, livestock and the environment; the industrial revolution has brought tremendous wealth to human society, but also for human beings. Categories bring catastrophic environmental pollution and destruction of ecological balance. New crop varieties with characteristics of high yield, high quality, virus resistance, insect resistance, cold resistance, drought resistance, waterlogging resistance, salt resistance and herbicide resistance can be cultivated by genetically modified technology, so as to reduce the dependence on pesticides, fertilizers and water, reduce agricultural costs, substantially increase the yield per unit area, improve the quality of food and alleviate the contradiction of food shortage in the world. But when a large number of genetically modified organisms enter the nature, they will probably cross with wild species and cause genetic pollution, which will affect the protection and sustainable utilization of biodiversity. The harm caused by this pollution to the environment and ecosystem is more difficult to eliminate than that caused by any other factors.
